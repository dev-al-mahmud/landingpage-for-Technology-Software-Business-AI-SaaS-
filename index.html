AI Research Synthesis
Based on 23 papers — Transformers & Attention Mechanisms
🔍 Core Finding
The Transformer architecture (Vaswani et al., 2017) represents a fundamental shift from recurrence-based sequence models to pure attention mechanisms. Subsequent work confirms this enables better long-range dependency modeling while improving parallelization and training efficiency by 3–8× vs. LSTMs.
📈 Research Trajectory
  • 2017–2019: Foundation models established (BERT, GPT-1/2). Bidirectional context emerges as key advancement.
  • 2020–2022: Scaling laws dominate. GPT-3 shows emergent capabilities. Efficient variants (LoRA, Adapter) reduce compute cost.
  • 2023–2024: Multimodal integration (GPT-4V, Flamingo). RLHF alignment becomes standard practice.
⚠️ Research Gaps Identified
Interpretability of attention patterns remains underexplored. 7 papers flagged energy efficiency at inference scale as a critical open problem. Hallucination mitigation lacks systematic benchmarks.
